Mentorship Meeting Guide for Mentors


Introduction


As a mentor at EarlyOn Meetings Inc., your insights and experiences are invaluable to guiding prospective students. This guide outlines potential questions organized by category to prepare you for one-on-one mentorship sessions.



Preparation and Conduct


  • Be prepared to discuss your high school and university experiences in detail.
  • Listen attentively to the mentee's questions, allowing them to speak first before providing your response.
  • Aim to provide concise, precise answers, efficiently managing your time to cover as much ground as possible.
  • You will have 65 minutes for the session, with an extra 5 minutes to conclude the discussion. However, you're free to end the meeting anytime after the 60-minute mark.

Potential Categories and Questions


Academic and Extracurricular Preparation

  • What projects did you undertake in high school to get admitted?
  • What strategies did you use to enhance your high school resume?
  • How did you spend your summer breaks during high school?
  • Does engaging in sports or playing an instrument impact admissions positively?
  • Did participating in summer programs or internships play a role in your admissions success?
  • How did you balance your academic workload with extracurricular activities?
  • Did you engage with any private admission consulting firms? If so, how was the experience?

Major and Career Path

  • Why did you choose your major, and how did you pursue your interest from an early stage?
  • Can you share any current university projects youâ€™re working on?
  • Do you have any advice for high schoolers aiming to enter your major?
  • What resources helped you decide on your major?
  • How early did you start exploring your current field of study?
  • What are the emerging trends or opportunities within your major?

Admissions Process

  • How did you approach writing your college essay? What topics did you choose?
  • What do you believe was the most impactful part of your resume?
  • Which other schools did you apply to, and where were you accepted?
  • How many iterations did your college essay go through before final submission?
  • What role did letters of recommendation play in your application?
  • How did you demonstrate your interest in your chosen university?

Campus Life and Advice

  • What advice do you have for adapting well as a future freshman?
  • Which residence hall do you recommend for making friends?
  • Are there any organizations or clubs you suggest joining?
  • What was your biggest challenge in adapting to university life?
  • Can you share tips on managing time effectively as a freshman?
  • What are the must-do experiences for a freshman at your university?

Specific Queries

  • What classes would you recommend taking in the freshman year?
  • Could you suggest strategies for crafting a standout college essay?
  • How important are AP classes or IB scores in the admissions process?
  • What are some misconceptions about the admissions process you encountered?
  • Is participating in the honors program or pursuing minors beneficial?

General Well-being and Resources

  • Where can freshmen find general help or academic support on campus?
  • Are there any easy-A classes or subjects better taken at community colleges and transferred?
  • How do you deal with academic stress or pressure at the university?
  • Are there any student-led initiatives or support groups you recommend?
  • What are the best ways to seek mentorship or academic advice on campus?

Sports and Extracurricular Activities

  • Can you provide information on sports teams or leagues available for students?
  • How do you balance academic responsibilities with athletic commitments?
  • Are there intramural sports teams, and how competitive are they?
  • Can involvement in music or arts groups aid in personal development or networking?
